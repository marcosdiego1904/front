### Pennsylvania LLC Structure
- ‚úÖ Operating as LLC (liability protection)
- ‚úÖ Tournament run under LLC entity
- ‚úÖ Proper business structure in place

### Tournament Classification
- **Type**: Skill-based contest (NOT gambling)
- **Rationale**: Memorization = skill, not chance
- **Legal precedent**: Spelling bees, trivia contests, esports
- **PA Compliance**: 18 Pa.C.S. ¬ß 5513 (skill predominates)

### Entry Model: Two Options
1. **$15 one-time entry fee** (tournament access only)
2. **$9.99/month Pro subscription** (tournament + all Pro features)

**Key Legal Point**:
- Both options have equal winning chances
- Pro subscription provides standalone value beyond tournament
- Tournament is ONE benefit of many (not sole reason to pay)
- Courts have upheld this model (see Duolingo, Fortnite precedent)

### Required Legal Documents

1. **Official Rules** (public, accessible):
   - Entry requirements
   - Eligibility (18+ OR parental consent)
   - Scoring system
   - Prize amounts
   - Disqualification terms
   - Liability limitations
   - Governing law (Pennsylvania)

2. **Terms & Conditions**:
   - Participants agree before entry
   - Checkbox: "I have read and agree to Official Rules"

3. **Prize Distribution**:
   - Prizes paid within 7 days of finale
   - IRS Form 1099-MISC for prizes $600+ (1st & 2nd place)
   - Winner must provide W-9 before payment
   - Winners responsible for taxes

4. **Liability Waiver**:
   - Release Lamp to My Feet LLC from liability
   - Technical failures not organizer's responsibility
   - Signed before participation

### Tax Implications

**For You (Organizer)**:
- Prize payments = business expense (deductible)
- File 1099-MISC for winners receiving $600+
- Track all payments meticulously

**For Winners**:
- Prizes = taxable income
- Must report on tax return
- You provide 1099 to help them comply

### What to Avoid

‚ùå Calling it "gambling" (use "skill-based contest")
‚ùå Allowing minors without parental consent
‚ùå Changing rules mid-contest
‚ùå Withholding prizes past stated timeframe
‚ùå Making winners based on luck/random draw

### Risk Mitigation

‚úÖ Publish Official Rules prominently
‚úÖ Be transparent about fees (show 50/50 split)
‚úÖ Use objective scoring (automated + judges)
‚úÖ Document everything (entries, scores, payments)
‚úÖ Have winners sign release forms before paying

**Legal Review**: Optional but recommended
- Cost: $500-1,500 for attorney review
- Not required for $625 prize pool
- Consider if scaling to $5,000+
